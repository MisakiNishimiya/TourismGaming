Adding GPS Coordinates to Your Tourism Packages

üìç Why Add GPS Coordinates?

When you add GPS coordinates to your packages, customers can:

  • See your location on the interactive map
  • Get accurate directions from their current location
  • Calculate exact distance and travel time
  • Find you more easily with navigation apps

üó∫Ô∏è How to Get GPS Coordinates

Method 1: Google Maps (Easiest)

1 Open Google Maps on your computer or phone

2 Search for your exact location (e.g., your resort, restaurant, or attraction)

3 Right-click (or long-press on mobile) on the exact spot

4 Click on the coordinates that appear (they look like: 7.0850, 125.9500)

5 Copy the numbers - these are your GPS coordinates!

üí° Tip: Make sure to click exactly on your entrance or main building for accuracy!

Method 2: On-Site with Your Phone

If you're physically at your location:

For Android:

  1. Open Google Maps
  2. Tap the blue dot (your current location)
  3. Your coordinates will appear at the top

For iPhone:

  1. Open the Compass app
  2. Swipe left to see your coordinates
  3. Or use Maps app and drop a pin on your location

‚úÖ GPS Coordinate Format

Correct Format:
  • Two numbers separated by a comma
  • First number is Latitude (North/South)
  • Second number is Longitude (East/West)
  • Example: 7.0850, 125.9500

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • ‚ùå Using degrees/minutes/seconds: 7¬∞ 5' 6" N
  • ‚úÖ Use decimal format: 7.0850
  • ‚ùå Reversing the numbers
  • ‚úÖ Latitude first, then longitude
  • ‚ùå Forgetting the comma between numbers
  • ‚úÖ Always include comma: 7.0850, 125.9500

To make sure your coordinates are correct:

3 Check if the pin drops on your location

4 If correct, use those coordinates in your package!
